Research At MIT

CLAIRITY: An Air Quality Network for MIT's Campus
As co-project manager, Paula helped lead a class of 20 seniors in designing, calibrating, and deploying 24 air quality sensors around the MIT campus. Each sensor node was equipped with the ability to measure CO, NO, NO2, O3, and particulate matter. Click here for video of the final CLAIRITY presentation!

NECAP: New England Climate Adaptation Project
Working under Professor Lawrence Susskind, Paula helped run simulations with members of the Cranston, RI community to better understand the effects of climate change on their decision-making processes. Paula also tabulated and summarized data from hundreds of simulation participants.

Professional Experience

World Bank Low-Carbon Liveable Cities Initiative
During a summer spent at the World Bank's Urban Anchor, Paula analyzed population and credit worthiness data for over 500 cities, helping her team to better understand the present status of urban landscapes on a global scale.

Air Liquide i-Lab
Upon graduating from MIT, Paula joined the team at the i-Lab in Paris, France. She worked on several projects, including: a study of the fifty top innovation clusters cites in the world; air quality industry market research; the i-Lab's Breathe in the City initiative; and a partnership with the MIT Senseable City Lab to analyze and visualize respiratory health data.

Stay Tuned

Venture for America
Starting in June 2015, Paula will be joining the ranks of Venture for America as a 2015 Fellow. She will spend two years in an emerging US city, both developing her business and entrepreneurship skills and participating in the process of urban regrowth.

Paula is eager to see how the private sector can contribute to urban redevelopment, and hopes to apply what she'll learn from this experience in other urban environments.
